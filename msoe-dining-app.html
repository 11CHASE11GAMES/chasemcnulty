Case Study

MSOE Dining Online Ordering App

A solo Interface Design project for an app that lets MSOE students order food from the dining hall ahead of time and pick it up quickly between classes.

Concept

Ordering food before the next class starts.

The app focuses on a fast student workflow: choose food, place the order, and pick it up without losing extra time during a busy class day.

Context

This was an individual project from my Interface Design class.

Role

I completed the full project myself, from concept to interface design.

Focus

Student needs, ordering flow, time savings, and clear mobile interface design.

Problem

Students often have limited time between classes. Waiting in line for food can make it harder to eat and still get to class on time.

Role

Because this was a solo project, I handled the full design process myself. I worked through the concept, user flow, interface layout, and visual design decisions.

Process

  • Defined the main user need: ordering quickly before class.
  • Mapped the core flow from browsing food to pickup confirmation.
  • Designed screens around speed, clarity, and simple decision making.
  • Focused the interface on actions students would need most often.

Outcome

The final concept was a mobile ordering experience built around a real student problem. It is relevant to my UX portfolio because it shows how I can identify a practical need, structure a clear flow, and design an interface around everyday use.
